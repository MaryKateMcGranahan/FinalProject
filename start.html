The Caesar Cipher

Julis Caesar was a Roman Emporer who lived from 100 BC to 44 BC. He was one of the first people to use encryption to secure messages. He sent messages to his army using a shifted alphabet. He shifted the alphabet by three, so a became d, b became e, and so on. Now, any encryption that involves shifting the alphabet by a certain number of letters is called a Caesar Cipher. The number of letters shifted by serves as they key to decode the cipher. Let's go through an example
