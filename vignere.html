The Vignere Cipher

If you know how a Caesar Cipher works. It is easy to break because, due to the fact it is a monoalphabetic cipher, there are only 25 possible answers. The Vignere Cipher is a monoalphabetic cipher and is more secure than a Caesar Cipher. Blaise de Vignere was a 16th century French cryptographer. He proposed this polyalphabetic cipher to King Henry III. Instead of using a number to shift the whole alphabet, the Vignere cipher requires a keyword. Each letter of the message is shifted by the corresponding letter of the keyword. Lets go through an example:
